⚠ Urgent Items Requiring Leadership Review
🚨 Accounting — Camera Surveillance Complaint
Multiple Accounting employees report being watched via cameras by management, creating an uncomfortable, distrustful environment.
This department scored 1.0 average from two respondents — the lowest in the survey.
Employees have asked for a more comfortable and trusting work environment free from constant surveillance.
🚨 Accounts Receivable — Supervisor Conduct
4 of 7 AR employees specifically describe supervisor behavior: excessive warnings, treating staff like children, undermining them in front of customers.
"Supervisor makes me feel dumb. What I say is always wrong."
Supervisor contradicts staff instructions with customers, damaging client relationships.
⚠️ Named Employee Issues — Accounting / AR
Multiple respondents (including Shipping) name specific Accounting/AR staff for errors, dishonesty, and rude behavior toward customers and coworkers.
Customers reportedly leaving or refusing to buy due to negative Accounting interactions.
Separate documentation recommended — names are in the raw data.
⚠️ Warehouse — Compensation Frustration
Warehouse employee: less than $1 raise in 2 years despite significant contributions. "It doesn't sit well with me."
Second respondent asks to bring back bonuses and address underperformers.
Flight risk — high-performing warehouse staff may leave if unaddressed.

Top Themes from Open-Ended Responses
💬
Communication Gaps
~9 mentions
Cross-department and leadership communication cited repeatedly. Employees want clearer expectations, regular meetings, and performance feedback. Suggestions ignored or require excessive documentation to be heard.
⚖️
Accountability & Fairness
~8 mentions
Rules not applied equally — leaders and favorites are protected while front-line staff face strict enforcement. Frustration with inconsistent policy and excessive warnings for minor issues.
💰
Compensation & Recognition
~6 mentions
Pay raises feel inadequate relative to contributions. Warehouse specifically flagged. Requests for written goals with real compensation tied to performance. Bonuses mentioned.
👥
Staffing & Workload
~5 mentions
Short-staffing creates scrambles and service lapses. Need buffer drivers/vehicles per large warehouse. Cross-training suggested (drivers ↔ warehouse). Underperformers slowing teams down.
📦
Parts Inventory & Pricing
~3 mentions
Sales reps and drivers report customers complaining about parts availability declining and prices exceeding dealerships. Losing clients as a result. New product orders increased but insufficient.
🔄
Change Fatigue
~3 mentions
Positive direction acknowledged but pace of constant change is stressful. Employees want projects finished before new ones start. "We should complete what we start, not leave projects half-done."
✓ Positive Signals & Momentum to Build On
SoCal Restructuring — Widely Praised
Multiple Sales respondents call out the new SoCal structure as one of the best decisions: "having the right people in the right positions is crucial."
Recent leadership changes in the past 2–3 weeks also viewed positively by Sales.
Company Direction — Generally Optimistic
Even lower-scoring respondents acknowledge the company is "heading in the right direction."
Purchasing (4.79) and IT (4.43) report high satisfaction across the board.
Customer service improvement noted over the last 1–2 years.
Standout Managers Recognized
Nerhit D. (Accounting) specifically praised: "very patient manager, every task explained step-by-step, all questions answered."
Purchasing cites strong trust and alignment with leadership direction.
Actionable Quick Wins Offered
Biweekly/monthly manager crew meetings to increase engagement.
Written branch goals. Paperless operations. Better driver tablets. PM delivery routes for underutilized drivers.
Low-cost, high-impact ideas worth evaluating immediately.
